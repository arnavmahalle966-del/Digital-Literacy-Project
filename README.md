# Digital-Literacy-Project
Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

In this task, I created a visual infographic using Canva to spread awareness about digital literacy. My design covered key topics such as digital literacy basics, safe internet practices, and email etiquette. I used icons, headings, and a clean layout to make the information easy to understand. This task helped me improve my design skills and learn how to present information visually.

Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio

I created and updated my profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. On GitHub, I made a profile README with my academic details and learning goals. On LinkedIn, I added my education details, and on Kaggle, I explored the platform for data-related learning. I also took screenshots of all profiles. This task helped me build a professional online presence.

Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

For this task, I used HackerRank to practice Python programming and completed a beginner-level challenge along with certification. I also created a Google Form quiz on digital literacy and viewed responses in Google Sheets. This task improved my coding and collaboration skills.

Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Communication

I wrote two professional emails—one requesting an assignment extension and another for a summer internship application. I also created a social media Do’s and Don’ts checklist and analyzed a situation of poor digital communication. This task helped me understand professional communication and online behavior.

Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

I created a case study on UPI/Online Payment Fraud, explaining how such scams happen and their impact. I also made a ‘Stay Safe Online’ checklist with prevention tips and included official reporting channels like cybercrime.gov.in and helpline 1930. This task increased my awareness of cybersecurity and online safety.
